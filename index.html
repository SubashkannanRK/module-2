Types of Animals

Carnivores

Small carnivores can include spiders, frogs and bats. Medium-size carnivores might include larger birds, such as eagles and hawks, snakes, and anteaters. Large carnivores range from wild dogs and wolves to large predators like lions, tigers or crocodiles

Omnivores

Some common mammalian omnivores include raccoons, opossums, skunks, pigs, rats, badgers, and most bear species. There are also several omnivorous birds, including chickens, crows, and robins. Some reptiles, such as lizards and turtles, are also omnivorous.

Herbivores

A herbivore is an animal anatomically and physiologically adapted to eating plant material.Herbivorous animals typically have mouthparts adapted to rasping or grinding. Horses and other herbivores have wide flat teeth that are adapted to grinding grass, tree bark, and other tough plant material.
